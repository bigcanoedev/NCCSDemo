Inuit worldview

Relationship with the land

Inuit relationship with wildlife is founded on respect, balance, and reciprocity. Taking only what is needed ensures the land remains healthy for future generations — this is Inuit Qaujimajatuqangit, lived knowledge carried through generations, not an external rule.

Extractive worldview

Maximum harvest logic

A resource-extraction worldview treats wildlife as commodity. The goal is to maximise harvest within legal limits. This perspective drove colonial resource practices in Nunavut, including the near-extinction of the bowhead whale through commercial whaling in the 1800s.

📖  Historical context

Commercial whalers depleted the bowhead whale to near-extinction within decades. Inuit, who had sustainably harvested bowhead for millennia, were forced to stop hunting for approximately 70 years — with profound cultural and food security consequences that persist today.
